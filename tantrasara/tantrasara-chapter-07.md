# Chapter Seven: The Path of Space

In the last chapter the whole of time was found folded inside the breath. This chapter does the same thing with space. It takes the entire universe — every world, every level of reality, the full vertical sweep from the densest earth to the highest Shiva — and finds it folded inside your own body. The tradition calls this the path of Space, and it is the natural companion to the path of Time. Time rests in the breath; space rests in the body.

## Space Is Not a Container Either

Just as you had to unlearn time as an empty container, you have to unlearn space the same way. You picture space as a vast emptiness that things sit inside of — and you picture yourself as one small thing, located at one small point, with the immensity of the cosmos stretching away from you on all sides, most of it impossibly far, most of it forever out of reach.

That picture is the bondage this chapter undoes. Space is not a container the Lord put you inside. Space is something the Lord is doing — the outward spreading of Consciousness into form, the way the one Light fans itself out into here and there, near and far, this place and that. Every location is a gesture of awareness taking a position. And because all of it is Consciousness spreading itself, none of it is truly outside you. The far galaxy and the near wall are both shapes within the one awareness that you are.

## The Map of Manifestation

To walk this path you need the map of how Consciousness spreads itself out, and the tradition gives it in layers.

At the most condensed level are the *tattva*-s, the reality levels — thirty-six rungs descending from pure undivided Consciousness at the top, through the powers of will and knowing and acting, down through the veiling forces that make a self feel small, down through mind and the senses, down through the elements, all the way to solid earth at the bottom. This is the spine of the cosmos: thirty-six steps by which the boundless Light contracts itself, stage by stage, into the dense, solid, separate-seeming world.

Spread across those reality levels are the *bhuvana*-s, the worlds — the countless particular realms and their inhabitants, the whole teeming variety of existence distributed up and down that vertical spine. And the *kalā*-s are the broad segments that gather these levels into great regions, like provinces on the map of being.

Do not get lost in the inventory. The point is not to memorize a cosmic atlas. The point is that all of it — every rung, every world, every region — is a single structure, and that structure is present, complete, in one place you can actually reach: your own body.

## Your Body Is the Universe

This is the heart of the chapter, and it is a stunning claim. The whole of that map — all thirty-six reality levels, all the worlds spread across them — is laid out within your own body. You are not a small thing located at one point in a vast external cosmos. You are a complete cosmos, folded into human form.

The solid earth-level lives in the densest part of you. The subtle levels of mind and perception live in the subtler reaches. The highest levels, pure awareness and its powers, live at the crown of your being. The entire vertical sweep from earth to Shiva runs through you, from the soles of your feet to the space above your head. The vital energy threads through all of it, weaving the whole structure together, inside and out.

So when the seeker on this path meditates on the body, they are not meditating on a lump of flesh. They are reading the map of all existence in the only copy they will ever hold directly. Every level of the universe has its seat somewhere in this form. To know the body truly is to know the cosmos, because the body is the cosmos, written small.

## Walking the Path Without Moving

Here is what makes this a path and not just a doctrine. The seeker ascends. They move their awareness, deliberately, up through the levels seated in their own body — beginning at the dense earth-level, rising through the subtler and subtler stages, climbing the inner spine of reality rung by rung, until attention rests at the highest level, where the body opens into pure Consciousness.

This is a journey through the entire universe that requires not a single step. You traverse all the worlds without leaving the place you sit. The pilgrimage is real, but it is vertical and inward — a climb up the ladder of your own being, which is the same as the ladder of the cosmos, because they were never two ladders.

And as the seeker climbs, the sense of being a small thing located at one point dissolves. How could you be small, when every level of existence is seated within you? How could you be located somewhere, when all the somewheres are arranged inside your own form? The climb itself unmakes the contraction. By the time attention reaches the summit, the seeker recognizes that they were never a point inside space. Space was always a spreading-out within them.

## Why This Path Matters

The fear this path addresses is the loneliness of the small self in the enormous cosmos — the ache of being one tiny, perishable speck adrift in an indifferent immensity. Most people carry this somewhere, the sense of being lost in a universe far too big to ever be home.

The path of Space answers it directly. You are not lost in the universe; the universe is found in you. You are not a speck in the immensity; the immensity is your own body, your own awareness, spread out and mistaken for somewhere else. When that recognition lands, the cosmos stops being a vast outside that dwarfs you and becomes your own form, intimate and whole. The fear of being small in a big world ends — not because the world got smaller, but because you discovered you were never the small thing inside it. You were the space in which all of it appears.

Here ends the seventh chapter of the Tantrasara, called The Path of Space.
