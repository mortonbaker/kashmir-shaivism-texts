# Chapter Two: The Pathless Path

In the first chapter I promised four ways home, and I named the first of them the pathless path. So before any method is described, let me speak about the way that is no way at all — the one the tradition calls Anupaya, which means, simply, "without means."

## When No Method Is Needed

Here is what happens. Sometimes grace descends on a person with such overwhelming force that the work is finished before it begins. Such a person hears the word of the teacher one time — just once — and turns it over in their own mind, and in that turning, sees. There is no practice to take up afterward. No technique to rehearse, no breath to follow, no thought to refine. The recognition simply stands, always awake, needing nothing to hold it in place.

This is the most intense form of the descent of grace. And for the one it falls on, no inner help and no outer help is required to realize that they are, and always were, Shiva.

## Why There Is Nothing To Do

You might object: if this person "sees" by turning the teacher's word over in their mind, then isn't that reasoning a method? Isn't thinking itself a technique?

Here is the answer, and it is the whole heart of this short chapter.

The Supreme Lord is your own Self. And that Self is self-luminous — it shines by its own light, the way the sun does not need a lamp held up to it to be seen. Now ask yourself: what could any method possibly accomplish here? A method is a way of getting from where you are to somewhere you are not yet. But you have never been anywhere other than your own Self. You cannot travel to the place you are already standing. You cannot use a flashlight to find the sun.

So the reasoning this person does is not a tool that produces a result. It is more like the last film clearing from an eye that could always see. Nothing is built. Nothing is added. A misunderstanding simply falls away, and what was always shining is noticed to be shining.

## Why This Comes First

This is why the pathless path is placed before all the others, even though almost no one enters here. It is placed first because it tells the truth about every path that follows.

Every method in the rest of this book — the path of will, the path of knowledge, the path of action — does exactly the same work this instant recognition does. None of them manufactures your freedom. None of them turns a bound soul into a free one. They only remove, by various means and at various speeds, the single mistake of taking yourself to be small. The methods exist because for most people the mistake is stubborn and needs to be worked loose gradually.

But here, in the pathless path, the mistake never even takes hold. The grace is so complete that there is nothing left to remove. And so this chapter is the shortest of all, because there is, quite literally, nothing to say about how to do a thing that requires no doing.

Here ends the second chapter of the Tantrasara, called The Pathless Path.
