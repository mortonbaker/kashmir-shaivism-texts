# Chapter Ten, Supplement: The Count of the Lanes, and the Thirty-Seventh

*This expands the main chapter on the Six-Fold Path. Read that one first. The main chapter gave the shape: the cosmos as one Path with six lanes — worlds, categories, and segments on the side of things; sentences, mantras, and primal sounds on the side of speech. This supplement gives the two pieces the spoken version set aside: Abhinavagupta's exact tally of how much falls within each of the five great segments, and his subtle teaching about a "thirty-seventh" level that does not run off into infinity. Both are in chapter ten itself, in his own summary verses — not, as one might assume, only in the larger Tantraloka.*

## The Five Segments, Defined Exactly

First, the five great segments (the *kalas*) are not vague zones; each covers a precise stretch of the thirty-six categories, and each is named for what happens in it:

- **Nivritti**, "cessation" — the segment of the **earth** category alone (the thirty-sixth), so called because the outward creation of categories *ceases* here, at the very bottom.
- **Pratishtha**, "foundation" — from **water** up through **primordial Nature** (categories thirty-five to thirteen), so called because, as cause, it *nourishes and fills*.
- **Vidya**, "knowledge" — from the **limited soul** up through **Maya** (categories twelve to six), so called because, as the objects thin out, *Consciousness predominates*.
- **Shanta**, "the peaceful" — from **Pure Knowledge** up through **Shakti** (categories five to two), so called because here the restless *waves of the sheaths grow calm*.
- **Shantatita**, "beyond the peaceful" — the **Shiva** category itself (the first), the segment that transcends even peace.

These five also correspond to the four great "eggs" of the previous chapter — the eggs of Earth, Nature, Maya, and Power — with egg-ness (the quality of being a bounded, resistant enclosure) extending all the way up to the Shakti category, where the last subtle "contact," the last faint resistance, is still found. Beyond all of it is the supreme free Principle, which, being utterly without objects, is called simply "beyond all segments."

## The Tally: How Much Lives in Each Segment

Here is the count Abhinavagupta sets down in three summary verses for his students — the exact distribution of the lanes across the five segments. Each segment holds a certain number of worlds (the coarsest object-lane), categories, and on the word-side, primal sounds, mantras, and sentences:

- **Nivritti** (earth): **16 worlds**, and one each of category-equivalent, primal sound, mantra, and sentence.
- **Pratishtha** (water to Nature): **56 worlds**, 23 categories and 23 primal sounds, 5 mantras, 5 sentences.
- **Vidya** (soul to Maya): **28 worlds**, 7 categories and 7 primal sounds, 2 mantras, 2 sentences.
- **Shanta** (Pure Knowledge to Shakti): **18 worlds**, 3 categories and 3 primal sounds, 1 mantra, 1 sentence.
- **Shantatita** (Shiva): **16 primal sounds**, with one each of sentence, mantra, and category.

Notice the shape of it. Down at the bottom, in the earth-segment, the world-count is high (sixteen) but the sound-count is minimal (a single primal sound) — gross reality is rich in *places* but poor in *subtlety*. At the very top, in the Shiva-segment, it inverts: a single world's worth of objectivity, but **sixteen** primal sounds — the subtlest reaches are poor in places and immensely rich in the seed-vibrations of sound. The cosmos is front-loaded with worlds at the bottom and with sound at the top, which is exactly what you would expect if, as the main chapter said, sound is the *inner* face and world the *outer* face of one Path.

(These are the numbers the main text's closing verses give. The fuller derivations — why 56, why 28 — are worked out at length in the Tantraloka; but the totals themselves are right here in chapter ten.)

## The Thirty-Seventh — and Why It Doesn't Run Forever

Then Abhinavagupta raises a subtle and beautiful puzzle. The thirty-sixth and highest category is Shiva, who by definition transcends everything. But the moment you *teach* about Shiva, or *meditate* upon Shiva, or *worship* Shiva, you have made Shiva into an object — something held in awareness, something pointed at. And whatever is held as an object is, in a sense, lower than the one holding it. So the Shiva you meditate upon is not quite the final word; there is a knowing awareness *beyond* him, taking him as its object. Call that a **thirty-seventh** level.

But then — does the same thing happen again? If you now meditate on *that* thirty-seventh awareness, have you not made *it* an object too, implying a thirty-eighth, and a thirty-ninth, and so on forever?

No, says Abhinavagupta, and this is the elegant resolution. The regress stops at the thirty-seventh. Because the thirty-seventh is precisely *unbroken Freedom itself* — the free, self-aware Consciousness that can be made the object of contemplation and yet never actually becomes a mere object, because its whole nature is to be the free subject. When you take *it* as what you meditate on, you are not pointing at one more thing in a series; you are resting in the very awareness that was doing all the pointing. There is nowhere further to go, because you have arrived at the one who was looking. The thirty-sixth (Shiva-as-category) is "transcending all categories"; the thirty-seventh (Shiva-as-free-subject) is where the counting simply comes to rest.

He adds a parallel three-fold scheme for the same insight: an "Self-segment" reaching up through the knowers still touched by latent limitation, a "Knowledge-segment" reaching up through the Lord-principle, and a "Shiva-segment" governing all the rest — and notes you can run the same logic through a nine-fold division, and so on. The number of divisions is a matter of how finely you choose to look; the destination is always the same single Freedom.

## The Essence

So the chapter's map is not left as a vague sketch. The lanes are counted — sixteen worlds here, fifty-six there, sixteen primal sounds at the summit — and the counting is shown to terminate, not in some highest object, but in the free awareness that was measuring all along. Which is the whole point of the six-fold path: you lay the entire cosmos out in exact lanes precisely so that you can walk it back, lane by counted lane, to the one Consciousness in which the counting, and the counter, and the counted, were never three.
