# Chapter Seven, Supplement: The Measured Cosmos Inside the Body

*This expands the main chapter on the Path of Space. Read that one first. The main chapter gave the vision — space is Consciousness spreading itself out, and the whole universe is folded into your own body, which you ascend without moving. This supplement is the actual map: why the map matters, the great nested "eggs" of manifestation with their real proportions, and the catalogue of worlds seated within the body. It is dense, and far more than the spoken version needs, but it is the literal cartography the practice walks.*

## Why You Have to Know the Map

Abhinavagupta opens with a warning that the main chapter left out, and it is the reason the whole technical apparatus exists. The seeker who knows the thirty-six principles must see the Consciousness that *transcends* them as Paramashiva — and must also see that this same Consciousness *contains* the entire universe. Both halves matter. Because if you don't hold the full map, you will stop too soon. You will grab some limited thing you can know — some state, some level, some radiant being — and mistake *it* for the Supreme. You might even take Vishnu or Brahma, who are real and exalted but are still only rulers *inside* Maya, for the highest reality.

That is the trap the map prevents. Knowing the cosmic procedure — the *prakriya* — is what keeps you from halting at a way-station and calling it home. You must know how high the structure goes so you don't worship a landlord and think you've met the owner.

## The Four Great Eggs

The universe is built as a nested series of "eggs" (*andas*) — vast enclosed spheres, each one wrapping the one below it, each immensely larger than the last. There are four, and they are stacked from densest to subtlest.

**The Earth Egg (Brahmanda).** The solid level. It is staggeringly vast — the text gives it as a hundred million units across — and inside it sits the entire familiar cosmos: the fire at the base of the worlds, the hells, the underworlds, the earth, the heavens, all the way up to the world of Brahma. Outside any single Earth Egg are a hundred Rudras, and the number of such eggs is itself beyond counting — countless cosmos-bubbles. Within this egg the levels expand by tens: water is ten times the earth, fire ten times water, then air, then ether, then the five subtle essences and the eleven powers of sense and action, up to the ego-principle. The intellect is a hundred times the ego.

**The Nature Egg (Prakriti-anda).** Above that, the principle of primordial Nature is a thousandfold, and it forms its own egg — and, like the Earth Egg, there are countless of these. Above Nature, the principle of the limited person (Purusha) is ten-thousandfold; then the power of limitation (Niyati) a hundred-thousandfold; and rising by tens through attachment, limited knowledge, limited time, and the principle of partial agency.

**The Maya Egg (Maya-anda).** Above those, Maya herself is ten-million-fold, and forms the third great egg — the enclosure of cosmic illusion, the membrane between the pure and impure worlds.

**The Power Egg (Shakti-anda).** Above Maya, the levels of the pure universe open out, each one enormously larger than the last: Pure Knowledge tenfold beyond Maya, the Lord-principle a hundredfold beyond that, the Ever-Shiva principle a thousandfold beyond that, and finally the Power-principle itself, vaster still — the fourth and highest egg. This Power is called the All-Pervading One, because she pervades the entire course of the universe, inside and out.

And above and through all four eggs is **Shiva** — immeasurable, beyond every course, pervading every course. The rule running through the whole structure is simple and worth holding: **the higher always pervades the lower; the lower is always pervaded by the higher.** Earth is wrapped in water, water in fire, and on up — every level enclosed and saturated by the one above it, until the whole of it, right up to the Power-principle, is enclosed and saturated by Shiva, who is enclosed by nothing.

## The Worlds Seated in the Body — the Five Regions

Across this vertical structure are distributed the *bhuvanas*, the worlds, each with its presiding lord — and the tradition gathers them into five great regions or *kalas*. When the seeker meditates on the body as the cosmos, these are the stations seated along the inner axis, from the soles upward.

**Nivritti — the region of cessation (Earth).** Sixteen worlds, beginning with the Fire-at-the-base and rising through a series of presiding lords up to Rudra, with the great serpent Ananta below as the support of all. This is the most external region, seated in the densest part of the body.

**Pratishtha — the region of foundation (Water up to Nature).** Seven sets of eight worlds — fifty-six in all. Among their famous names are the great pilgrimage-lords: Bhairava, Kedara, Mahakala, Shrishaila, Harischandra, Gokarna, Mahalaya, Avimukta. These octets rise through water, fire, air, the ego, the intellect, and into Nature. (It is worth noticing that the holiest pilgrimage sites of the outer world are here mapped as *inner* stations — the geography you would travel to is already standing inside you.)

**Vidya — the region of knowledge (the limited Person up to Maya).** Twenty-eight worlds. In the level of the Person stand eleven Rudras, beginning with Vama; then pairs of worlds in the levels of limited knowledge, partial agency, and necessity; three in limited time; and eight in Maya, the "night." This is the region where the soul is most knotted into limitation.

**Shanta — the region of peace (the pure universe).** Eighteen worlds. Five in Pure Knowledge, the eight Lords-of-Knowledge in the Lord-principle, and five in the Ever-Shiva principle. Here the worlds grow serene, close to the source.

**Shantatita — the region beyond peace (Power and Shiva).** Beyond even worlds — the abode of the Power-principle and Shiva, where the map runs out because there is nothing left to map. This is not a place among places; it is the silence the whole structure was pointing at.

## Walking It, and Dissolving It

The point of laying out this immense scaffold is not to memorize it but to *climb and then collapse it.* The practice the main chapter described is exactly this: the seeker takes the whole measured path — all the eggs, all five regions, all the worlds — and dissolves it inward, step by step. The entire cosmic path is dissolved into the body; the body into the breath; the breath into the intellect; the intellect into the great void; and the void into pure Consciousness. And there, seeing the Supreme in his own awareness, by sheer fullness the seeker becomes Bhairava.

So the map is built only to be folded up. You learn the whole vast architecture precisely so that you can recognize every floor of it as seated in your own form, draw it all back into yourself, and discover that the immensity you thought you were lost inside was your own Consciousness, spread out and briefly mistaken for somewhere else.
