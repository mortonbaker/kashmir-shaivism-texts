# Chapter Eight, Supplement: The Five Powers, the Double Nature, and the Sheaths

*This expands the main chapter on the Nature of the Categories. Read that one first. The main chapter rendered the heart of chapter eight: the two kinds of causation (real and invented), the "screen" of the Lord's doership on which all apparent cause-and-effect is painted, and the doctrine of Totality. Those are Abhinavagupta's own terms. But the chapter then continues into a dense second half the spoken version sets aside — how the categories actually unfold, the five powers, the "double nature" of every category, and the machinery of the sheaths. This supplement gives that. It is technical, and meant for reading.*

## The Five Powers Become the First Five Categories

The Supreme Lord is full of five powers: Consciousness, Bliss, Will, Knowledge, and Action. He does not lose these when he manifests a universe; instead he lets each one step forward and predominate in turn, and as each takes the lead, a category is born at the very top of the cosmos.

- When **Consciousness** predominates — pure self-aware light — that is the **Shiva** category, the first.
- When **Bliss** predominates — the Light delighting in itself — that is the **Shakti** category.
- When **Will** predominates — the first lean toward "I shall be a universe," holding knowledge and action in perfect balance — that is the **Sadashiva** category.
- When **Knowledge** predominates — the universe clearly envisioned, still as oneself — that is the **Ishvara** category.
- When **Action** predominates — the universe ready to pour outward — that is the **Pure Knowledge** (Sadvidya) category, the fifth.

These five are the **pure path** — the levels where the Light, however much it expands, never once forgets that all of it is itself. Each has its presiding lord (Shiva, Shakti, Sadashiva, Ishvara, Ananta), and each has a class of perfected perceiver who lives at that level: the Shambhavas, the Shaktas, the Great Lords of Mantra, the Lords of Mantra, and the Mantra-beings. Across all five, Shiva himself is directly the Doer.

## The Turn Downward: Ananta and the Impure Path

Below the pure path, the categories from six to thirty-six — the **impure path**, the ordinary world of limited selves and solid things — are not emanated by Shiva directly. They are brought forth by **Ananta** (also called Aghoresha), acting by the Lord's Will, and brought forth for a specific reason: so that the countless individual souls, who carry within them an urge to taste a limited, agitated kind of experience, can have a world in which to enjoy it.

That urge itself is worth naming. It is a vibration whose whole content is the feeling of *not being full* — the sense, false but insistent, that something is missing. That feeling is the seed of the entire descent into limitation.

## The Three Stains

What contracts the boundless Self into a small soul are three "stains" or impurities — not moral dirt, but three progressively grosser forgettings:

- The first and subtlest is simply the **Will to hide one's own infinite Self** — the deep impurity of feeling incomplete, of taking oneself to be a limited atom rather than the whole. Abhinavagupta is careful to say this hiding is itself *real*, a genuine act of the Lord's freedom, not an illusion-in-the-cheap-sense.
- From that follows the stain of **differentiated perception** — seeing a world of genuinely separate objects.
- And grossest, the stain of **action and its consequences** — karma, the web of deeds and their fruits that drives the variety of worldly experience.

A soul in whom the first stain is "about to be destroyed" stands just above Maya, no longer feeding karma; a soul still fully wrapped in it churns onward through the variety of transmigration. And the agitation of even this deepest stain happens only by the force of the Lord's Will — because anything genuinely inert is, by itself, powerless to do anything at all.

## Maya, and the Double Nature of Everything

Then comes **Maya** — here the sixth category, the material cause of the impure world. She is eternal (she belongs to the Lord, who is eternal), inert (the things she will produce are themselves inert), and all-pervading (she pervades all her effects). One must not confuse this Maya-*category* with **Maya-***shakti*, which is something quite different — the Lord's own Absolute Freedom, the living power that manifests the Maya-category in the first place.

Here Abhinavagupta states one of the chapter's deepest theses: the **double nature** (*dvairupya*) of every category from Kala (the seventh) down to Earth (the thirty-sixth). Each such category has two faces — an inner face, where it lives as an aspect of Consciousness, and an outer face, where it stands forth as an apparently separate object. His teachers gave a clean proof: *whatever shines within a thought also stands outside it, separately and evidently, with a form of its own — as a pot does.* What you can think, you can also meet as an object; the inner and outer faces are two aspects of one reality. (This is why even great adepts whose realization ranges over Maya, Kala, Nature, and intellect are genuinely perfected beings — those levels are real territory, not mere error.)

## The Sheaths Unfold

From Maya, then, the universe is generated. In the deepest truth this generation is *without succession* — all at once — but viewed through the invented causation of the main chapter, it appears to unfold in order. And it unfolds differently for each individual soul (though the Lord's Will can also unify a group of souls, the way an audience is unified by one performance).

The first thing born from Maya is **Kala** — not time here, but the sheath of *limited agency*. It takes the soul, who has been as if in deep sleep, utterly contracted, and rouses it to a little doership — the first swelling of "I can act," the faint return of the infinite power to do, now throttled to a trickle. From Kala the rest of the sheaths and instruments follow in turn, building out the whole apparatus of a limited person in a limited world — the full descent that the companion supplement to chapter nine ("The Descent of the Thirty-Six") lays out rung by rung.

Whether this whole apparatus of sheaths becomes a **cage or a ladder** depends on one thing: the descent of the Lord's grace. Turned by grace toward the Lord, the very same Kala-and-the-rest produce effects that carry the soul toward its own true nature, opposing the current of transmigration — they become pure. Left to themselves, they bind. And what makes the difference — the descent of Power, *shaktipata* — is the subject Abhinavagupta turns to a few chapters on.

## The Essence

So the nature of the categories, fully unfolded, is this: the one Light, by letting each of its five powers lead in turn, becomes the five pure categories; then, through Ananta and the three stains and the freedom-power called Maya, freely contracts itself into the sheaths and instruments of countless limited souls — every one of those categories keeping a double face, inner and outer, Consciousness and object, at once. Nothing in the whole descent is ever anything *but* the Light. The categories are not bricks; they are poses. And the entire point of naming them so precisely is so that the soul can recognize, in each level of its own apparent limitation, the infinite attribute it was carved from — and climb back.
