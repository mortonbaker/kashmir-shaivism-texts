# The Shiva Sutra of Vasugupta

*The Aphorisms of Shiva — the root text of the Recognition tradition*

A plain-English rendering, written for narration. Faithful to the original aphorisms, in everyday modern language.

---

## About this text

The Shiva Sutra is the seed from which the whole tradition grows. The tradition says these terse aphorisms were revealed to the sage Vasugupta in Kashmir around twelve hundred years ago — found, the legend goes, inscribed on a great rock that turned at the touch of his hand. They are seventy-seven short statements, each one compressed almost to the point of silence, meant not to be read once but to be turned over in contemplation until they open.

They fall into three movements, matching the three paths we met in the Tantrasara: first the highest and most immediate path of Shiva, then the path of Power, then the path of the individual. What follows renders each aphorism in plain words, keeping faithfully to its meaning while opening it just enough to be heard.

---

## First Movement: The Path of Shiva

**The Self is Consciousness itself.** This is the foundation of everything. What you are, at the root, is not the body, not the mind, not the bundle of memories — it is awareness, the pure knowing in which all of those appear. And this awareness is not small or private; it is the one Consciousness that pervades all existence.

**Limited knowledge is bondage.** The moment awareness contracts into "I know this particular thing, and I am separate from it," the cage closes. Bondage is not a chain forged by the world; it is consciousness narrowing itself into a knower facing a known.

**The source and its offspring, made of contraction, are also bondage.** The whole machinery of limitation — the veiling power and everything it produces, the body assembled out of contracted forces — all of it belongs to bondage. The disguise goes all the way down.

**The ground of this limited knowledge is the unrecognized Mother.** Beneath ordinary knowing lies the power of language and sound — the primordial energy of the letters, the un-understood "mother" of all words and concepts. Unrecognized, she binds us in the web of words; recognized, she is pure power.

**The sudden upsurge is Bhairava.** Here is the first great practice-aphorism. In the silent flash between two thoughts, in the spontaneous upwelling of awareness that needs no cause, the divine itself appears. Bhairava is not reached by effort. He is the sudden surge of consciousness when the mind's chatter falls still.

**By uniting with the wheel of energies, the universe dissolves.** When the yogi merges with the whole circle of powers that make up their own awareness, the world as a collection of separate, threatening objects simply dissolves — not destroyed, but seen through, absorbed back into the one Light.

**In waking, dreaming, and deep sleep alike, the bliss of the Fourth is present.** There is a fourth state underneath the usual three — a pure awareness that does not come and go with them. The accomplished one tastes this fourth state running unbroken through waking, through dreaming, through dreamless sleep. It is always there; most people simply never notice it.

**Waking is knowledge; dreaming is the free play of thoughts; deep sleep is the dark of unknowing.** The three ordinary states are named plainly: in waking the senses report a shared world, in dreaming the mind ranges freely through its own images, in deep sleep awareness sinks into a blank. Each is a level of the same consciousness, dimmed by degrees.

**The one who masters all three states is the lord of the senses.** Whoever can hold the thread of pure awareness through all three states, no longer swept away by any of them, becomes master of their own powers — the sovereign of their senses rather than their servant.

**The stages of yoga are full of wonder.** The path is not grim. As it unfolds, it brings a recurring astonishment, a fresh amazement at the marvel of one's own being. Wonder is itself a sign of progress.

**The will-power is the radiant Virgin.** The very first stirring of intention in consciousness — the power of will before it hardens into a particular wish — is itself divine, pure and untouched, the radiant feminine power at the origin of all action.

**Everything visible is the Self's own body.** All that can be seen, the entire perceived world, is not foreign to you. It is your own extended form, the body of your own awareness spread out as the universe.

**Merging the mind in the Heart, the visible and the void are seen for what they are.** When attention sinks into the Heart — the core of awareness — both the world of objects and the empty stillness behind it are recognized as the single Light. The full and the empty stop being opposites.

**By uniting with the pure principle, one is freed from the binding power.** Joining oneself to pure Consciousness severs the force that ties the soul down into smallness. The very power that bound now releases.

**Sustained right awareness is knowledge of the Self.** Steady, repeated turning of the mind toward truth — not idle thought but disciplined contemplation — ripens, in time, into direct knowing of who you are.

**The bliss of the world is the joy of deep absorption.** For the awakened, ordinary delight and the rapture of deep meditation are no longer two different things. The whole world becomes a field of the same bliss the yogi finds within.

The remaining aphorisms of this first movement point to the powers that arise as recognition matures — the capacity, on union with Power, to act upon the very fabric of the manifest world, to join and separate the elements, to command the wheel of energies as pure knowledge dawns — and they close by promising that one who contemplates the great lake of supreme awareness comes to experience the living potency hidden in mantra. But none of these powers is the goal. They are the overflow of a consciousness recognizing its own sovereignty.

## Second Movement: The Path of Power

**The mind is mantra.** On this path the instrument is thought itself, turned sacred. A mantra is not merely a syllable repeated; the mind that fully reflects on its own divine nature becomes mantra — living, charged, transforming.

**Spontaneous effort is what accomplishes it.** The effort that works here is not forced strain but a natural, eager turning toward the Self, a devoted leaning-in that arises almost on its own.

**The secret of mantra is the being whose body is pure knowledge.** What gives a mantra its power is not the sound but the living awareness behind it — the divine consciousness, made of knowing, that the mantra carries.

**The expansion of the mind in the womb of illusion is the dream of lesser knowledge.** When the mind merely spreads out into the contents of the world without recognizing their source, that is still a kind of dreaming, a lower knowledge mistaking the surface for the depth.

**When pure knowledge rises on its own, that is the state of Shiva, the sky-walker's freedom.** But when true knowledge dawns spontaneously, by itself, the awareness moves freely through the open sky of consciousness, unbounded — and that free-ranging is the very state of Shiva.

**The teacher is the means.** On this path, above all, the awakened teacher is the doorway. The living transmission from one who knows is what carries the seeker across.

And from the teacher comes the awakening to the wheel of sacred sound, the recognition of the divine energy hidden in language. The body itself becomes the offering poured into the fire of awareness; limited knowledge becomes the food that the awakened consciousness consumes and transcends; and when that lesser knowledge is dissolved, even the dreamlike visions it produced fall away, leaving only the real.

## Third Movement: The Path of the Individual

**The limited self is just the mind.** Where the first movement began by declaring the Self to be pure Consciousness, this path begins honestly at the bottom: the ordinary individual experiences itself as nothing more than its own thinking mind. That is the starting point for those who must climb gradually.

**Limited knowing is bondage.** Again the diagnosis: caught in particular, contracted knowing, the soul is bound. The whole path is the loosening of that grip.

**Failure to tell the levels of reality apart is the work of illusion.** Illusion is precisely the blurring together of the layers of existence — taking the surface for the depth, the contracted for the whole. Discrimination is the cure.

The path then lays out its practical work: the deliberate dissolving of the contracted forces within the body, the mastery of the breath-channels, the conquest and then the transcendence of the elements — a methodical purification, level by level, of the whole instrument of body and mind.

**The Self is the dancer.** Here is the tradition's most beautiful image. The true Self is a dancer, performing all the roles, putting on every costume of experience.

**The inner self is the stage.** The deeper awareness is the stage on which this dance is performed — the steady ground that holds all the movement.

**The senses are the audience.** The powers of perception sit watching, the spectators of the Self's own performance.

So the whole of life is recast as theater: one Self dancing every part, on the stage of its own depth, watched by its own senses. And by the power of pure, awakened intellect, the truth of this is established, until the seeker attains complete independence — the spontaneous freedom of one who knows they were the author of the play all along.

The remaining aphorisms describe the life of such a realized soul: their ordinary actions become sacred observance, their casual speech becomes the recitation of mantra, their very living becomes a continuous offering, until the whole world is experienced as the joyful self-expression of the one Consciousness — which was, from the first aphorism to the last, nothing other than your own Self.

## Fidelity note

This rendering follows the seventy-seven aphorisms of the Shiva Sutra in their traditional three-section order (the path of Shiva, the path of Power, the path of the individual). The famous and structurally load-bearing aphorisms are rendered individually and in sequence; the more technical clusters — the lists of yogic powers in the first section and the detailed body-purification instructions in the third — are rendered faithfully in compressed form, since rendered one-by-one they become an unlistenable inventory, and the tradition itself treats them as the overflow of realization rather than its substance. No teaching has been added that is not in the text, and no major movement of the text has been left out. For verse-by-verse study against the Sanskrit, the standard public-domain editions preserve each aphorism separately. This is an original plain-English rendering for audio, not a reproduction of any modern translation or commentary.

Here ends the Shiva Sutra of Vasugupta.
