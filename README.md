# Kashmir Shaivism Texts

Plain-English audiobook renderings of the foundational texts of non-dual Kashmir Shaivism (Trika).

These are written to be **read aloud** — from a teleprompter, into a microphone — in clear, modern English. They use everyday metaphors a contemporary listener can follow, and they deliberately avoid the dense brackets, diacritics, and technical apparatus of scholarly translations. The goal is faithfulness to the meaning, set free for the ear.

These renderings are **not** translations. They do not reproduce any existing translator's text. They are fresh prose retellings of the underlying teachings, written from the ancient (public-domain) source verses so the ideas can be heard and understood by a general modern audience. Fidelity to the source is verified separately, chapter by chapter, against the public-domain Sanskrit — the audio files themselves are kept clean of notes and apparatus.

## Two-tier structure (Tantrasara)

The main `tantrasara/` chapters are the **lean, listenable** versions, sized for narration. The `tantrasara-supplementary/` folder holds the **fuller, comprehensive** versions for *reading* — the deeper material Abhinavagupta includes that would overload the spoken version (cosmologies, tattva-derivations, the detailed inner maps). Each supplement is keyed to its chapter (e.g. `chapter-07-supplement.md`). Read the main chapter first; go to the supplement when you want the full picture.

## How to read the collection

A natural sequence for a newcomer:

1. **Heart of Recognition** — the whole system in twenty short steps; the best doorway.
2. **Shiva Sutra** — the root aphorisms the tradition is built on.
3. **Spanda Karika** — the pulse of consciousness; the vibration teaching.
4. **Virupaksha Panchashika** — the universe as the body of Shiva; the Recognition teaching in fifty verses.
5. **Vijnana Bhairava Tantra** — the practice manual: 112 meditation techniques you can actually do, with cross-references to the Tantrasara.
6. **Tantrasara** — the full system: paths, cosmos, and the rituals of initiation and worship.
7. **Shivastotravali** — Utpaladeva's hymns; where the whole philosophy melts into devotion and song.

## Future Documents of Study (roadmap)

Texts and themes queued for the same plain-English treatment, so the thread isn't lost:

- **[Paratrishika Vivarana](./paratrishika-vivarana/)** — Abhinavagupta's own commentary on the Paratrishika. The deepest source on **how the universe unfolds from sound**: the phonemes from *a* to *kṣa* as the contraction-and-expansion of Consciousness, vowels as Shiva's inner states, consonants as the unfolding world. The primary text for the mātṛkā (alphabet-emanation) doctrine. *(Folder created; to be built out.)*
- **The six courses (ṣaḍadhvan), in depth** — how manifestation is laid out along the sound-side (varṇa / mantra / pada) and the object-side (kalā / tattva / bhuvana). Master treatment is **Tantraloka chapters 6, 7, and 11** (with Jayaratha's *Viveka* commentary); the Tantrasara chapters here are the digest. Modern key: André Padoux, *Vāc: The Concept of the Word*.
- **Possible later additions** — Spandanirṇaya (Kshemaraja on the Spanda); Paramarthasara (Abhinavagupta's 105-verse summary); Shivastotravali commentary (Kshemaraja).

## A note on the tradition

Non-dual Kashmir Shaivism holds that there is only one reality — pure Consciousness, called Shiva — and that everything that appears, including every individual self, is that one Consciousness freely taking form. Bondage is simply the forgetting of this; liberation is its recognition. The texts here are maps and methods for that recognition.
