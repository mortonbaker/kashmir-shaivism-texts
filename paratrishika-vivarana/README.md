# Paratrishika Vivarana

*Abhinavagupta's commentary on the Paratrishika — the deepest source on how the universe unfolds from sound.*

**Status: placeholder. To be built out.**

This folder is reserved for a plain-English, audiobook-style rendering of the **Paratrishika Vivarana** (Parātrīśikāvivaraṇa), Abhinavagupta's great commentary on the short scripture called the Paratrishika ("the supreme [teaching] of three" — the trident of Shiva, Shakti, and the individual).

## Why this text

The Tantrasara and Tantraloka lay out *that* the universe emanates as sound (the mātṛkā, the wheel of letters from **a** to **kṣa**). The Paratrishika Vivarana is where Abhinavagupta actually *works it out* in detail:

- the vowels as Shiva's own internal states (Anuttara *a*, Icchā *i*, Unmeṣa *u*, and the rest);
- the consonants as the unfolding of the manifest world;
- how Consciousness, by knowing itself, pours out the entire alphabet of creation and recognizes the whole universe as its own self-utterance;
- the supreme heart-mantra and its meaning.

It is the natural companion to the alphabet-emanation passage compressed in **Tantrasara chapter 3** (the reflection doctrine and the mātṛkā), and the primary text behind the "world from sound" question.
