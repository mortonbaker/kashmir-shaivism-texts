# The Vijnana Bhairava Tantra

*The Wisdom of Bhairava — a hundred and twelve doorways into the awakened state*

A plain-English rendering, written for narration. Each meditation practice is explained as an actual, doable technique, and cross-referenced with the Tantrasara where the two texts illuminate each other.

## About this text

The Vijnana Bhairava Tantra is the most beloved practice-manual of the tradition: a hundred and twelve meditation techniques (*dharanas*), given as an intimate dialogue between the Goddess and Bhairava — Shakti and Shiva, the two faces of the one Consciousness. Where the other texts map the territory, this one hands you the keys. It asks for almost nothing external — just your breath, your senses, your attention, turned in the right way — and finds the doorway to the infinite hidden inside the most ordinary moments.

The master theme running through the practices is *centering*: resting in the gap, the middle, the space between — between two breaths, two thoughts, two moments. Bhairava's promise is that you need not master all hundred and twelve. Established firmly in even one, the awakened state is yours.

## How this rendering works

Each practice is numbered and rendered as a technique you could actually try, in plain modern language faithful to its source verse. Where a practice connects to the Tantrasara — the breath-and-time work, the dissolution of the elements, the path of will, the liberating thought — the connection is drawn, so the field-manual and the map illuminate each other.
